I am a multilingual scholar of technology, media, and aesthetics trained in continental philosophy and critical theory in Europe and the United States. My research is broadly concerned with the social ontology and politics of technical systems, with particular attention to the ways in which modern technologies embody and uphold social structures and power relations. Proceeding from the premise -- shared across much of continental philosophy of technology -- that our technical engagements with the world are constitutive of, rather than external to, human culture and institutions, my work combines historical analysis of technological and discursive formations to uncover the social imperatives inscribed in the material infrastructures of the modern world.

In my doctoral research, I have focused on the _longue durée_ of industrial modernity and the machine-mediated ordering of life in highly technologized society, tracing the roots of our contemporary condition to the nineteenth century. My dissertation, “Karl Marx, Philosopher of Technics: Critique, Alienation, and the Materiality of Social Power,” grounds this historical inquiry in Marx’s engagement with the industrial literature of his time, reconstructing -- against prevailing productivist and economicist readings -- how he theorized the relationship between capitalism and the modern technical system. Though largely misunderstood by the Marxist tradition itself, Marx’s account of the social logic underpinning industrialization presciently identified in the capitalist subsumption of the labor process the basis of the technocratic rationality that presently governs the relationship between humans and technics.

I hold a Ph.D. from Duke University’s Graduate Program in Literature, where I specialized in critical theory and media philosophy, as well as a Fulbright-funded master’s degree in media studies from New York University. I completed my undergraduate training in Spain, where I hold two degrees in philosophy (UNED) and fine art (University of Barcelona). I am currently an affiliated researcher with the Historical Materialism Studies Group of the Societat Catalana de Filosofia, the philosophy section of the Institut d’Estudis Catalans, the highest academic body for the Catalan language and culture. My work has appeared in the South Atlantic Quarterly and the Marx and Philosophy Review of Books.
