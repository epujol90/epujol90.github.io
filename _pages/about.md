I am a multilingual scholar trained in continental philosophy and critical theory in Europe and the United States. Working at the intersections of philosophy of technology, media studies, and aesthetics, I am broadly interested in the social ontology and politics of technical systems, with particular attention to the ways in which modern technologies embody and uphold social structures and power relations. Proceeding from the understanding -- shared by much of continental philosophy of technology -- that our technical engagements with the world are constitutive of, rather than external to, human culture, institutions, and even consciousness, my research seeks to uncover the social logics inscribed in the technologies of the modern world and the way they frame, channel, and mediate human agency.

In my doctoral research, I have focused on the _longue durée_ of industrial modernity and the social predicaments of life in highly technologized society, tracing the roots of the contemporary condition to the nineteenth century. My dissertation, “Karl Marx, Philosopher of Technics: Critique, Alienation, and the Materiality of Social Power,” grounded this historical analysis in Marx’s engagement with the industrial literature of his time, examining the relationship between capitalism and modern technical evolution, and how the capitalist subsumption of the labor process gave rise to the technocratic logic presently organizing the relationship between humans and technics.

I hold a Ph.D. from Duke University’s Graduate Program in Literature, where I specialized in critical theory and media philosophy, as well as a Fulbright-funded master’s degree in media studies from New York University. I completed my undergraduate training in Spain, where I hold two degrees in philosophy (UNED) and fine art (University of Barcelona). I am currently an affiliated researcher with the Historical Materialism Studies Group of the Societat Catalana de Filosofia, the philosophy section of the Institut d’Estudis Catalans, the highest academic body for the Catalan language and culture. My work has appeared in the South Atlantic Quarterly and the Marx and Philosophy Review of Books.
