I am a scholar of technology, media, and aesthetics trained in continental philosophy and critical theory in Europe and the United States. I am currently an affiliated researcher with the Historical Materialism Research Group of the Societat Catalana de Filosofia (Institut d’Estudis Catalans). I am writing a monograph on Marx's technological studies and researching the history and politics of automation, artificial intelligence, and the social ontology of computational machines.

I hold a Ph.D. from Duke University’s Graduate Program in Literature, where I specialized in critical theory and media philosophy, as well as a Fulbright-funded master’s degree in media studies from New York University. I received my undergraduate training in Spain, where I completed two degrees in philosophy (UNED) and fine art (University of Barcelona). My work has appeared in _South Atlantic Quarterly_ and the _Marx and Philosophy Review of Books_, with further articles in preparation for _Historical Materialism_, _Theory, Culture & Society_, and _Qui Parle_. Alongside my research, I have substantive experience in writing pedagogy, including two years of writing center practice at Duke’s Thompson Writing Studio and teaching writing-intensive courses on media studies, philosophy of technology, and the history and politics of AI.
