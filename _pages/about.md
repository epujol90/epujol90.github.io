I am a scholar of technology, media, and aesthetics trained in continental philosophy and critical theory in Europe and the United States. My research examines the social ontology of technical systems as constitutive of -- rather than external to -- human agency, culture, and institutions, with particular attention to the ways the material infrastructures of the modern world embody and uphold relations of power and domination. 

In my doctoral research, I have focused on the _longue durée_ of industrial modernity and the machine-mediated ordering of life in technologized society, tracing its roots to the nineteenth century. My dissertation, “Karl Marx, Philosopher of Technics: Critique, Alienation, and the Materiality of Social Power,” grounds this historical inquiry in Marx’s engagement with the industrial literature of his time, reconstructing -- against prevailing productivist and economicist readings -- how he theorized the relationship between capitalism and modern technological transformations. Though largely misunderstood by the Marxist tradition, Marx’s account of industrial development, I argue, presciently identified in the capitalist subsumption of the labor process the basis of the technocratic illusion that governs the relationship between humans and technics in advanced industrial society -- and which finds its latest expression in the contemporary AI project and its pretense of expanding human autonomy through the automation of thought itself.

I hold a Ph.D. from Duke University’s Graduate Program in Literature, where I specialized in critical theory and media philosophy, as well as a Fulbright-funded master’s degree in media studies from New York University. I received my undergraduate training in Spain, where I completed two degrees in philosophy (UNED) and fine art (University of Barcelona). I am currently an affiliated researcher with the Historical Materialism Studies Group of the Societat Catalana de Filosofia -- the philosophy section of the Institut d’Estudis Catalans, the highest academic body for the Catalan language and culture. My work has appeared in _South Atlantic Quarterly_ and _Marx and Philosophy Review of Books_, with further articles under review at _Historical Materialism_ and _Theory, Culture & Society_, and an essay in preparation for a special issue of _Qui Parle_.
